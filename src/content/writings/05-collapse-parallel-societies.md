We live in an era of profound institutional failure. The response from existing institutions has been either denial or acceleration — and the wealthy are constructing private, parallel systems while public services wither.

Against this backdrop, bioregional organizing offers neither utopian vision nor dystopian retreat, but pragmatic response. Not exit from collapsing systems, but transformation through patient rebuilding from the ground up — rooted in irreducibly local material reality, and tied together cosmolocally across bioregions.
