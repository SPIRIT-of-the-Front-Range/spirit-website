The bioregional movement faces a fundamental paradox: the very institutions needed to coordinate collective action at scale are structurally incompatible with the principles of ecological embeddedness and distributed autonomy that define the bioregional vision.

This paper presents a dual-structure model that addresses this tension — a "membrane institution" that interfaces with legacy legal and financial systems, paired with a bioregional network that operates through credibly neutral coordination mechanisms. The framework draws on Elinor Ostrom's work on commons governance and contemporary innovations in distributed coordination.
